Cameron Lewis

Screenwriter

I’ve been inspired by the muckraking work of Michael Moore in films such as Bowling for Columbine and Where to Invade Next and the revolutionary work that Spike Lee put forth for in representing the depth and complexities of the black community in America, like Do the Right Thing and BlacKkKlansman. Spike Lee’s words about how people “need to find their own stories” has inspired me to go out of my comfort zone and explore the world to seek out the stories that have traditionally not had a voice. These directors have managed to successfully tackle very sensitive topics in a respectful and informative way, and I am striving to do the same as well. The medium of film is a powerful tool to convey my message of equality and awareness to my audience. My Digital Media Academy has taught me how to productively use film techniques in order to incorporate the slight and almost unnoticeable subtleties of what makes a film so much more powerful and poignant. I hope to use the techniques I’ve learned and further develop them so that I can take the next step in preparing for a career in the film industry.
